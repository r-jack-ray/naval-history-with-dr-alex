# Similarity Groups

Assign one primary rough group from the demonstrated keyed sense. Use title and alias relations, normalization provenance, entity-family membership, evidence-text similarity, and repeated co-keying only as routing signals. Never group by popularity, key count, alphabetic position, current summary quality, or SEO value.

Keep close relatives adjacent: general family before specific members, class before named members when useful, and conflict before its campaigns, battles, and operations. Record secondary relations without duplicating ownership. Put context-poor subjects in a broad generic or miscellaneous group. Mark unrelated multi-sense slugs `blocked-taxonomy`.

Use this sequence:

1. Naval vessels and ship families
2. Weapons, sensors, and combat systems
3. Aviation and air power
4. Military services, organizations, bases, and people
5. Wars, campaigns, battles, and operations
6. Strategy, doctrine, tactics, command, and intelligence
7. Logistics, industry, procurement, and maritime economics
8. States, geography, politics, diplomacy, and law
9. Land warfare and general military history
10. Historical periods, societies, and ideas
11. Science, engineering, technology, and research
12. Books, media, games, fiction, and culture
13. Broad generic groups
14. Miscellaneous and uncertain

Do not force naval framing onto ancient or modern history, land warfare, aviation, politics, economics, industry, logistics, railways, science, research, culture, games, or fiction when the evidence demonstrates another primary sense.
